Trigonometric Functions

f(x) = a(sin(b(x-h))) + k

This is the general equation of a sine wave.

  • a represents the vertical stretch of the sine wave.
  • b represents the wavelength of the sine wave.
  • h represents the horizontal shift of the sine wave.
  • k represents the vertical shift of the sine wave.

f(x) = a(cos(b(x-h))) + k

This is the general equation of a cosine wave.

  • a represents the vertical stretch of the cosine wave.
  • b represents the wavelength of the cosine wave.
  • h represents the horizontal shift of the cosine wave.
  • k represents the vertical shift of the cosine wave.
